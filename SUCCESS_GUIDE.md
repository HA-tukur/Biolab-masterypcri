# DNA Extraction Success Guide

## Mission A: Clinical Biopsy (Animal Tissue)

### Success Criteria
- **Concentration**: 200-1200 ng/µL
- **Purity (A260/280)**: ≥ 1.7
- **Verification**: BOTH Nanodrop AND Gel required
- **Missed Reagents**: 0
- **Protocol Adherence**: Critical

### Detailed Step-by-Step Protocol

#### Step 1: Tissue Disruption
1. **Select Method**: Enzymatic Digestion (recommended for biopsy)
2. **Equipment Needed**: None special
3. **Action**: Click "Enzymatic Digestion" button
4. **Result**: Gentle digestion preserves DNA integrity

#### Step 2: Proteinase K Digestion
1. **Equipment Needed**: Incubator
2. **Reagent**: Proteinase K (from kit)
3. **Volume**: 2µL EXACTLY
4. **Actions**:
   - Click 2µL button in pipette control
   - Click bouncing pipette above tube to dispense
   - Click MIX button (REQUIRED - affects purity!)
   - Load tube into incubator
   - Set temperature to 56°C (use + button to adjust)
   - Click START to incubate
5. **Critical**: Must MIX and must incubate at 56°C ± 5°C

#### Step 3: Lysis Phase
1. **Reagent**: Lysis Buffer (from kit)
2. **Volume**: ~500µL (can use 500µL or 1000µL)
3. **Actions**:
   - Click 500µL button in pipette control
   - Click bouncing pipette to dispense
   - Click MIX button (REQUIRED - affects purity!)
   - Load tube into centrifuge
   - Click SPIN button
4. **Critical**: Must MIX and SPIN for proper lysis

#### Step 4: Binding/Column Load
1. **Reagent**: Binding Buffer (from kit)
2. **Volume**: ~500µL
3. **Actions**:
   - Click 500µL button
   - Click pipette to dispense
   - Click MIX button (REQUIRED - affects purity!)
   - Load tube into centrifuge
   - Click SPIN button
4. **Critical**: Must MIX before spinning, must SPIN

#### Step 5: Wash Stage
1. **Reagent**: Wash Buffer (from kit)
2. **Volume**: ~500µL
3. **Actions**:
   - Click 500µL button
   - Click pipette to dispense
   - DO NOT MIX (mixing causes DNA loss!)
   - Load tube into centrifuge
   - Click SPIN button
4. **Critical**: DO NOT mix, but must SPIN

#### Step 6: Elution
1. **Reagent**: Elution Buffer (from kit)
2. **Volume**: **20µL for BEST quality** (30µL acceptable, 50µL lower quality)
3. **Actions**:
   - Click 20µL button (this is now directly in pipette control)
   - Click pipette to dispense
   - Load tube into centrifuge
   - Click SPIN button
4. **Critical**: 20µL gives highest concentration

#### Step 7: Verification
1. **Nanodrop**:
   - Click "Use Nanodrop" button
   - Follow simulation steps (open, clean, blank, load 1µL, measure)
2. **Gel Electrophoresis**:
   - Click "Run Gel" button
   - View gel result showing DNA bands
3. **Critical**: BOTH verifications required for pass/mastery

### Common Failure Reasons

1. **Low Concentration** (< 200 ng/µL):
   - Used too much elution volume (> 30µL)
   - Missed spins (didn't centrifuge when required)
   - DNA loss in wash (mixed when shouldn't have)

2. **Low Purity** (< 1.7):
   - Didn't mix in steps 2, 3, or 4
   - Mixed in step 5 (wash stage)
   - Missed spins
   - Started purity: 1.88
   - Penalty for not mixing steps 2/3/4: -0.3
   - Penalty per missed spin: -0.4
   - Penalty for mixing in wash: -0.2

3. **Protocol Compromised**:
   - Wrong temperature for incubation (not 56°C ± 5°C)
   - Volume significantly off target (> 50µL difference for non-proteinase steps)
   - Missed reagents

4. **Unverified**:
   - Didn't use both Nanodrop AND Gel

## Mission B: Cassava Leaf (Plant Tissue)

### Success Criteria
- **Concentration**: 200-350 ng/µL (narrower range than Mission A!)
- **Purity (A260/280)**: ≥ 1.7
- **Verification**: BOTH Nanodrop AND Gel required
- **Missed Reagents**: 0
- **Protocol Adherence**: Critical

### Detailed Step-by-Step Protocol

#### Step 1: Tissue Disruption
1. **Select Method**: Manual Grinding (required for plant tissue)
2. **Equipment Needed**: Mortar & Pestle, Liquid Nitrogen
3. **Actions**:
   - Purchase mortar & pestle and liquid nitrogen from procurement
   - Click "Manual Grinding" button
4. **Result**: Plant cell walls broken down
5. **Note**: Proteinase K step is SKIPPED when using manual grinding

#### Step 2: Lysis Phase
1. **Reagent**: Lysis Buffer (CTAB or kit buffer)
2. **Volume**: ~500µL
3. **Actions**:
   - Click 500µL button
   - Click pipette to dispense
   - Click MIX button (REQUIRED - affects purity!)
   - Load tube into centrifuge
   - Click SPIN button
4. **Critical**: Must MIX and SPIN

#### Step 3: Binding/Column Load
1. **Reagent**: Binding Buffer
2. **Volume**: ~500µL
3. **Actions**:
   - Click 500µL button
   - Click pipette to dispense
   - Click MIX button (REQUIRED - affects purity!)
   - Load tube into centrifuge
   - Click SPIN button
4. **Critical**: Must MIX and SPIN

#### Step 4: Wash Stage
1. **Reagent**: Wash Buffer
2. **Volume**: ~500µL
3. **Actions**:
   - Click 500µL button
   - Click pipette to dispense
   - DO NOT MIX (mixing causes DNA loss!)
   - Load tube into centrifuge
   - Click SPIN button
4. **Critical**: DO NOT mix, but must SPIN

#### Step 5: Elution
1. **Reagent**: Elution Buffer
2. **Volume**: **50µL for BEST quality** for Mission B
3. **Actions**:
   - Click 50µL button
   - Click pipette to dispense
   - Load tube into centrifuge
   - Click SPIN button
4. **Critical**: 50µL is optimal for Mission B (not 20µL like Mission A!)

#### Step 6: Verification
1. **Nanodrop**: Click "Use Nanodrop"
2. **Gel Electrophoresis**: Click "Run Gel"
3. **Critical**: BOTH required

### Key Differences from Mission A

1. **Manual Grinding** instead of Enzymatic Digestion
2. **Proteinase K step is SKIPPED**
3. **Concentration Range**: 200-350 ng/µL (much narrower!)
4. **Optimal Elution Volume**: 50µL (not 20µL)
5. **Purity Calculation**: Step 2 mixing is not checked (manual grinding counts as disruption)

### Final Tips

1. **Read Protocol Book**: Click the Success Criteria button before starting
2. **Procure All Items First**: Get everything before entering lab
3. **Follow Exact Volumes**: Use correct µL amounts
4. **MIX When Required**: Steps 2, 3, 4 need mixing (except step 2 in Mission B manual)
5. **DON'T Mix Wash**: Step 5 must NOT be mixed
6. **Use Optimal Elution**: 20µL for A, 50µL for B
7. **Complete Both Verifications**: Nanodrop AND Gel required
8. **Temperature Matters**: 56°C for Proteinase K incubation

### Troubleshooting

**Q: Why did I fail with concentration 150 ng/µL?**
A: Used too much elution volume or missed spins. Use 20µL for Mission A, 50µL for Mission B.

**Q: Why did I fail with purity 1.5?**
A: Didn't mix in required steps (2, 3, 4) or mixed in wash step (5), or missed spins.

**Q: Why did I get "unverified" status?**
A: Didn't complete both Nanodrop AND Gel electrophoresis.

**Q: Why did I get "protocol compromised"?**
A: Incubation temperature was wrong, or volume was significantly off target.
